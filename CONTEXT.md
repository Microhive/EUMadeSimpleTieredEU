# Tiered Europe

Tiered Europe is an editorial scenario for showing different depths of European integration. The context exists to explain how countries and partners may sit on a shared path toward, within, or alongside the European project.

## Language

**Tiered Europe Scenario**:
An editorial model of Europe organized into concentric integration tiers.
_Avoid_: Official EU proposal, prediction

**Integration Tier**:
A named level of participation in the **Tiered Europe Scenario**.
_Avoid_: Group, bucket, category

**Inner Union**:
The deepest **Integration Tier** for states willing to share common defence, eurozone depth, Schengen, and foreign policy.
_Avoid_: Core EU, first tier

**European Union**:
The full membership **Integration Tier** with shared EU law, budget, institutions, representation, and the single market.
_Avoid_: EU when referring to the whole European project

**Associate Membership**:
A bridge **Integration Tier** for candidates, close neighbours, and partners aligned with a path toward the single market or full membership.
_Avoid_: Waiting room, partial EU

**European Community + Friends**:
The widest **Integration Tier** for democratic partners cooperating on security, climate, energy, crisis response, and supply chains.
_Avoid_: Community, Friends

**Country**:
A state or territory placed on the map within the **Tiered Europe Scenario**.
_Avoid_: Flag, feature

**Capability**:
A policy area or shared obligation associated with an **Integration Tier**.
_Avoid_: Feature, badge

**Integration Ladder**:
The idea that a **Country** may move inward through **Integration Tiers** as trust, reforms, capacity, and public consent grow.
_Avoid_: Permanent label

**Tier Arrangement**:
The current placement of **Countries** across **Integration Tiers** in the **Tiered Europe Scenario**.
_Avoid_: Tier state, assignment map

## Relationships

- A **Tiered Europe Scenario** contains exactly four **Integration Tiers**
- An **Integration Tier** contains zero or more **Countries**
- A **Country** belongs to at most one direct **Integration Tier** at a time
- A **Capability** belongs to one or more **Integration Tiers**
- A **Tier Arrangement** records which **Countries** currently belong to each **Integration Tier**
- The **Integration Ladder** describes movement from **European Community + Friends** toward **Inner Union**

## Example Dialogue

> **Dev:** "If Ukraine is in **Associate Membership**, should it also appear in **European Union**?"
> **Domain expert:** "No. Its direct **Integration Tier** is **Associate Membership**, but the scenario should still make the inward path through the **Integration Ladder** clear."

## Flagged Ambiguities

- "EU" can mean the **European Union** tier or the broader European project; use **European Union** only for the full membership tier.
- "Community" is a short UI label for **European Community + Friends**; use the full term in domain code and documentation.
