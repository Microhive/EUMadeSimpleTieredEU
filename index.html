European integration, made visible

What could a tiered EU become?

A multi-speed Europe could let willing states integrate faster while keeping a clear path for candidates, neighbours, and democratic friends to move closer to the European project.

Scenario lens

Integration as a ladder, not a wall

The outer tier keeps the democratic community visible; the inner tier shows where deeper defence, eurozone, Schengen, and foreign policy integration could move first.

01

Open doors

Associate membership can make progress tangible before full accession.

02

Shared standards

Closer tiers should still orbit the same democratic and legal values.

03

Strategic weight

Security, energy, climate, and crisis response can reach beyond Europe.

04

Forward motion

Countries can move inward as capacity, trust, and public support grow.

This is an editorial scenario, not an official EU proposal. Source anchors: EU countries, EU enlargement, EU values, and World Atlas map data.
